# Civic Leverage: Community Engagement Playbook

## Deep Canvassing Framework

*   **The Opening:** "Hi, my name is [Your Name] and I'm a neighbor here in Des Moines. I'm talking with folks today about how we can work together to clean up our neighborhood parks and protect our local public spaces."
*   **The Story Share:** "When large, out-of-state investment companies buy up local properties and let them fall apart, it hurts all of our families. It forces housing prices up and makes it harder for kids to have safe spaces to play outside, which creates an unnecessary strain on our community."
*   **The Bridge:** "A lot of folks feel that our city leaders are ignoring the issue because the problem keeps getting passed back and forth between different state and city offices. But we have a real opportunity to fix this if we pack the room at the upcoming City Planning Board meeting."
*   **The Action Call:** "Would you be willing to sign this quick public comment sheet to demand that the board enforces city code violations on these corporate owners?"

## Targeted Message Outlines

### Message for Municipal Board Members
> "As a resident of Des Moines, I am urging you to utilize your administrative authority to enforce strict local code inspections on properties held by out-of-state LLCs. Our neighborhood data shows that neglect from these corporate entities strains local infrastructure and compromises safety. Fulfilling your regulatory mandate to hold these owners accountable will instantly protect local housing stability and support Iowa families."

### Message for Neighborhood Association Leaders
> "Please consider adding corporate property accountability and local zoning reviews to the agenda for our next neighborhood meeting. By coordinating our public comments and documenting infrastructure issues together, we can place direct, organized pressure on city code enforcement teams to do their jobs and protect our community assets."
